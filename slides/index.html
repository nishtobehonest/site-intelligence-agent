The Business Problem

The Gap

Field technicians (HVAC, electrical, plumbing) make high-stakes decisions under time pressure. The data they need exists — manuals, OSHA docs, past job records — but is scattered across binders in the truck and CRMs back at the office.

This is not a data availability problem.

The AI Problem

Why AI?

Too many documents, too many versions, too many cross-references to do manually at scale. A technician cannot cross-reference OSHA 1910.147, a 2019 vs. 2023 Carrier manual, and 50 past job records in real time. AI can.

Why Not Just Prompt ChatGPT?

  • No access to proprietary job history or equipment records
  • No source citations — no way to verify the answer
  • No confidence routing — answers even when it should escalate
  • Hallucination risk on technical specs (wrong refrigerant pressure = danger)

Why RAG?

Retrieval-Augmented Generation grounds the model's output in retrieved, citable documents — not parametric memory. The corpus is the source of truth.

Consistency Testing · A/B Design · Preference Optimization

Consistency Testing

Same 5 queries (one per equipment type) run 3× in independent sessions. Semantic similarity measured between outputs.

Target: <15% variance on factual queries. High variance triggers a retrieval audit.

GenAI equivalent of residual analysis — catches instability before a user does.

A/B Test Design

Variant A: Answer + citations only. No confidence label.

Variant B: Answer + HIGH / PARTIAL / LOW + explicit uncertainty language.

Hypothesis: Surfacing uncertainty increases appropriate reliance. Technicians decide better when to act vs. escalate.

Grounded in human factors research on calibrated AI reliance.

Preference Optimization

Every dispatcher override is logged: query, system answer, override reason code.

These logs become preference pairs for DPO fine-tuning on Llama 3 70B in v2.

The feedback loop is built in from day one — not retrofitted.

The eval framework today is the training data pipeline for the next model version.

Guardrails · AI Concept Map

Four Guardrails (Every Response)

  1. Citation requirement — no response without source doc + collection + relevance score
  2. Hallucination prevention — system prompt: "If uncertain, say so. Do not fabricate procedures."
  3. Safety flagging — electrical/refrigerant answers get an additional warning regardless of confidence level
  4. Scope boundary — off-topic queries get a graceful decline, not a hallucinated answer

In safety-critical contexts, a confident wrong answer is more dangerous than an unnecessary escalation. The system is calibrated accordingly.

Under ~500 users: Claude API wins on simplicity and total cost.

At scale (>500 users): Llama 3 70B self-hosted with DPO fine-tuning wins. Migration = one environment variable change — the system is model-agnostic by design.

Strategic path: Launch on Claude → accumulate dispatcher override pairs → fine-tune Llama 3 70B at 500+ users → DPO drops per-query cost by 10–20×.
